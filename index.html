01 / Problem

We have protocols for connection.
Not for truth.

The Internet was built to exchange information — not to verify it. Every AI system, every platform, every database has its own proprietary view of truth. None of it is independently verifiable.

01
AI systems hallucinate because there is no decentralised truth layer.
Large models predict plausibility, not truth. Without independent consensus on semantic correctness, confident falsehoods propagate at machine speed.
02
Existing consensus orders transactions — not their semantic quality.
Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake answer "which block is next?" They are silent on "is the claim inside it correct?" Ordering ≠ validation.
03
MCP and A2A connect AI systems. Nobody proves the content is true.
Agent-to-agent protocols solve technical interoperability. They do not answer the more fundamental question: has this knowledge been independently verified?
04
The EU AI Act mandates audit trails. No decentralised infrastructure satisfies this.
Articles 9, 13 and 17 require cryptographically robust, tamper-evident records for high-risk AI outputs. Enforcement: 02 August 2026. No decentralised solution exists — until now.
02 / Protocol

How AgentsProtocol works.

Three deterministic phases. Every accepted block carries a cryptographic quality certificate that any node can re-derive and any observer can challenge.

1
Submit — A validator receives a claim
Any participant submits a knowledge claim A = (data, signature). The knowledge corpus K is versioned and content-addressed — its hash is fixed in the genesis block.
claim = {data: d, σ: signature(d, private_key)}
2
Validate — S_con and Ψ are computed
Each validator computes the Semantic Consistency Score S_con against corpus K and solves k canonical dummy claims to generate an error vector. The Ψ statistic proves independence.
S_con(A) = max(0, (cos(v_A, v̄_κ) − τ) / (1 − τ))
Ψ = 1 − Σwᵢwⱼ|ρ(eᵢ,eⱼ)| / Σwᵢwⱼ
3
Anchor — Block accepted into the GHOSTDAG DAG
If S_con ≥ θ_min and Ψ ≥ Ψ_min, the block is admitted. Block weight determines canonical ordering. Accepted knowledge is permanently anchored — tamper-evident, globally available.
W(B) = Ψ_B · Σ_{A∈B} S_con(A)
